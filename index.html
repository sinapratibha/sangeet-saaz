Raag Bhairav

Swar:

  • Aaroh: Sa Re (Komal) Ga Ma Pa Dha (Komal) Ni Sa'
  • Avaroh: Sa' Ni (Komal) Dha Pa Ma Ga Re (Komal) Sa

Pakad: Sa Re (Komal) Ga Ma, Dha (Komal) Ni Sa'

Thaat: Bhairav

Jati: Sampurna–Sampurna

Time: Early morning (1st prahar – dawn)

Raag Bhoopali

Swar:

  • Aaroh: Sa Re Ga Pa Dha Sa'
  • Avaroh: Sa' Dha Pa Ga Re Sa

Pakad: Sa Re Ga, Pa Dha Sa'

Thaat: Kalyan

Jati: Audav–Audav

Time: Evening (7 PM – 9 PM)

Raag Yaman

Swar:

  • Aaroh: Ni (Teevra) Re Ga Ma (Teevra) Dha Ni Sa'
  • Avaroh: Sa' Ni Dha Pa Ma (Teevra) Ga Re Sa

Pakad: Ni Re Ga Ma, Dha Ni Sa'

Thaat: Kalyan

Jati: Sampurna Sampurna

Time: Night (9 PM midnight)

Raag Tilak Kamod

Swar:

  • Aaroh: S R G P N S'
  • Avaroh: S' N D P M G R S

Pakad: G P N S' N D P, G R S

Thaat: Khamaj

Jati: Audav–Shadav

Time: Second prahar of the night (9 PM – 12 AM)

Theory

Click to learn about Indian classical music theory

🎵 Indian Classical Music Theory

  • Swar (Notes): Sa, Re, Ga, Ma, Pa, Dha, Ni
  • Types of Swar: Komal (flat), Teevra (sharp), Shuddh (natural)
  • Saptak: Mandra (low), Madhya (middle), Taar (high)
  • Raag: A melodic structure with rules, used for expression
  • Thaat: Parent scale that classifies raags (10 main Thaats)
  • Taal: Rhythm patterns like Teentaal (16), Jhaptaal (10)
  • Alankar: Pattern exercises to improve control and pitch

Practice

Click on a time of day to view your practice routine:

Morning Routine

Morning Routine (6 AM - 9 AM):

  • Warm-up: 15 minutes of simple alankars (Sa Re Ga Ma).
  • Raag Practice: 30 minutes of Raag Yaman (focus on Aaroh-Avaroh).
  • Vocalization: 10 minutes of sa re ga ma with a drone.
  • Breathing Exercises: 10 minutes of pranayama (deep breathing).
Afternoon Routine

Afternoon Routine (12 PM - 3 PM):

  • Vocal Exercises: 20 minutes of humming exercises for voice control.
  • Raag Practice: 30 minutes of Raag Bhairav, focusing on emotional expression.
  • Alankar: 15 minutes of alankars, adding variations for practice.
  • Rhythm Practice: 10 minutes of taal practice using Teentaal.
Evening Routine

Evening Routine (6 PM - 9 PM):

  • Warm-up: 15 minutes of sa re ga ma (slow tempo).
  • Raag Practice: 45 minutes of Raag Bhoopali or Raag Marwa (focus on improvisation).
  • Alankar: 15 minutes of alankars with different pitches and rhythms.
  • Performance Practice: 15 minutes of practice with instrumental accompaniment.
